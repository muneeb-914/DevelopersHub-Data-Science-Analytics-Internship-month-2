# Task 5: Interactive Business Dashboard in Streamlit

## Objective
Build an interactive Streamlit dashboard for analyzing sales, profit, discount behavior, and segment-wise business performance from the Global Superstore dataset.

## Dataset Used
The dashboard and notebook use `data/Global_Superstore2.csv`.

- Rows: 51,290
- Columns: 24 raw (expanded to 28 via feature engineering)
- Unique orders: 25,035
- Unique customers: 795
- Date range: 2011-01-01 to 2014-12-31
- Regions: 13
- Categories: Furniture, Office Supplies, Technology
- Missing values: `Postal Code` has 41,296 missing values; this field is not used in the dashboard metrics
- Duplicate rows: 0

The bundled `data/Global_Superstore2.xlsx` is kept as the Excel copy of the same dataset.

## Dashboard Features
| Panel | Chart Type | Business Question |
|---|---|---|
| KPI Row | Cards | What are total sales, profit, orders, and average discount? |
| Sales Trend | Dual-axis bar + line | How do monthly sales and profit move over time? |
| Region Donut | Donut chart | Which regions contribute the most revenue? |
| Sub-Category Profit | Horizontal bar | Which product groups are profitable or loss-making? |
| Segment Comparison | Grouped bar | Which customer segment drives sales and profit? |
| Top 5 Customers | Horizontal bar | Who are the highest-sales customers? |
| Discount Impact | Bar chart | At what discount levels does margin turn negative? |
| YoY Trend | Multi-line chart | How do categories perform by year? |
| Raw Data Table | Filtered table | What rows are behind the selected filters? |

## Approach
1. Load `Global_Superstore2.csv` and parse `Order Date` / `Ship Date` with day-first formatting.
2. Create `Year`, `Month`, `Quarter`, and `Profit Margin %` fields.
3. Validate missing values and duplicate rows.
4. Build EDA views in `notebook/superstore_eda.ipynb`.
5. Build an interactive Streamlit dashboard in `app.py` with filters for region, category, sub-category, segment, and year.

## Key Findings
1. Technology leads total sales and profit, with about $4.74M in sales and $663.8K in profit.
2. Overall weighted profit margin is about 11.61%.
3. Discounts above 20% are loss-making overall: 21-30% has about -5.53% margin, and 31%+ has about -51.27% margin.
4. Consumer is the largest segment by sales and profit; Home Office has the highest weighted margin.
5. Tables are the main loss-making sub-category at the aggregate level, with about -$64.1K profit.
6. Q4 is the strongest sales quarter in each year, with 2014-Q4 the highest quarter overall.

## Business Recommendations
- Cap or tightly approve discounts above 20%.
- Prioritize Technology growth while protecting margins in Office Supplies.
- Review Tables pricing, supplier cost, and discount rules.
- Use dashboard filters to locate region or segment-specific margin problems.
- Prepare inventory and operations for Q4 demand peaks.
